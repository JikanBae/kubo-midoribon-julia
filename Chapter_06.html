logistic function $$ q_i = \mathrm{logistic}\left(z_i\right) = \frac{1}{1 + \exp\left(-z_i\right)} $$ $$ z_i = \beta_1 + \beta_2 x_i + \cdots $$

Distribution.jlLogistic()Wikipedia

$$f\left(x; \mu, \theta\right) = \frac{1}{1 + \exp\left(-\frac{x-\mu}{\theta}\right)}$$

$\exp$$-z_i = -\frac{x-\mu}{\theta}$$\beta_1 + \beta_2 x_i = \frac{1}{\theta}x_i - \frac{\mu}{\theta}$$\beta_1 = -\frac{\mu}{\theta}$, $\beta_2 = \frac{1}{\theta}$$\mu = -\beta_1\theta$, $\theta = \frac{1}{\beta_2}$

6.7 正規分布とその尤度¶

$$ p\left(y | \mu,\sigma\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}}\exp\left\{-\frac{\left(y-\mu\right)^2}{2\sigma^2}\right\} $$

Normal(,)

The Normal distribution with mean and standard deviation 0 has probability density function

$$ f(x; \mu, \sigma) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2 \pi \sigma^2}} \exp \left( - \frac{(x - \mu)^2}{2 \sigma^2} \right) $$

Note that if == 0, then the distribution is a point mass concentrated at . Though not technically a continuous distribution, it is allowed so as to account for cases where may have underflowed, and the functions are defined by taking the pointwise limit as $ 0$.

Normal(mu)        # Normal distribution with mean mu and unit variance
Normal(mu, sig)   # Normal distribution with mean mu and variance sig^2
